About Jharkhand

The Land of Forests, Waterfalls, Temples, and Tribal Heritage

History & Culture

Jharkhand, carved out of Bihar in 2000, is known as the 'Land of Forests'. It has a rich history of tribal heritage and resistance movements that played a role in India’s freedom struggle. With 32 tribal communities, Jharkhand is home to vibrant festivals, dances, music, and handicrafts.

The cultural landscape is enriched by folk traditions, art forms, and rituals that celebrate harmony with nature.

Nature & Wildlife

Jharkhand boasts stunning natural beauty with more than 50 waterfalls, dense forests, hills, and national parks. Betla National Park, Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary, and Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary are home to elephants, tigers, leopards, and diverse flora and fauna.

The state’s breathtaking landscapes make it a paradise for eco-tourism and adventure seekers.

Major Attractions

Waterfalls

Famous waterfalls like Hundru, Dassam, and Jonha offer spectacular views and picnic spots.

Temples

Spiritual destinations such as Baidyanath Dham, Parasnath Hills, and Jagannath Temple draw millions of pilgrims.

Tribal Culture

Experience traditional tribal lifestyles, handicrafts, and cultural festivals that reflect centuries of heritage.

Local Cuisine

Dive into authentic dishes like Dhuska, Litti-Chokha, Rugra, and Handia that represent the flavors of Jharkhand.

Why Visit Jharkhand?

Where nature meets culture – Jharkhand offers waterfalls, temples, tribal heritage, wildlife adventures, and authentic food, all in one destination.
